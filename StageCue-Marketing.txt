BODY:
Background: I run AV for a small church and kept finding
vMix too complex and expensive for what I needed. OBS is
great for streaming but overkill for just playing videos
on an LED screen. So I built something simpler.

StageCue is a media controller specifically for live
events — churches, weddings, concerts, school productions.
The entire workflow is: load media → preview → CUT to LED.

Tech stack: Electron app, Windows + Mac.

What it does:
- Dual preview/output window (like vMix but simpler)
- Auto-detects HDMI display and opens fullscreen
- CUT, FADE, WIPE transitions
- Playlist with video thumbnails, drag to reorder
- Save/reload playlists
- Audio device routing

What it doesn't do (intentionally):
- No streaming/recording (use OBS for that)
- No live camera input
- No graphics/overlay system

$39 one-time with a 14-day free trial.
Website: stagecue.pro

Genuinely curious what the church AV community thinks
is missing. Happy to add features that make weekly
services easier to run.
